Humanoid Robot for Firefighting

Foot-terrain interaction control for humanoid robots in firefighting scenarios

Project Period: 2023.07 – 2024.12 Funding: Beijing Municipal Science & Technology Plan (北京市科技计划) Role: PhD Student Lead

Overview

Developed advanced foot-terrain interaction walking control algorithms for humanoid robots operating in firefighting scenarios, addressing the fundamental contradiction between computational constraints and gait quality.

Key Contributions

  • Designed a hierarchical planning-control framework: “Mapping-Optimization-Reconstruction”
  • Proposed dimensionality-reduced manifold motion modeling for efficient gait representation
  • Developed safe trajectory optimization algorithms for terrain-adaptive walking
  • Achieved stable omnidirectional walking on stairs, slopes, and low obstacles
  • Published 1 first-author paper in Q1 TOP journal

Technical Highlights

  • Manifold projection for dimensionality reduction of gait space
  • Trajectory library construction with efficient reconstruction algorithms
  • Perception-guided planning and control for unstructured terrain
  • Project management: led milestone reviews, technical reports, and acceptance testing
